# Day 14 — Coding Mock #1 + Week 2 Spaced Review

> **Timebox: ~3 hours.** Coding mock (60m, hard stop) → Mock review (30m) → Week 2 weak-topic review (60m) → Reflection (30m).
> The point of a mock is **finding gaps under pressure**, not solving every problem. Don't peek at solutions during the 60m.

---

## 1. Coding Mock #1 — Self-Administered

Pick **two unseen problems** from your weak categories (use your `#review/day-XX` tags). Suggested mix:
- 1 Medium from a topic you struggled with (Graph, DP, Backtracking, Sliding Window).
- 1 Hard you've never seen.

**Suggested problem bank** (don't pre-read; pick blind from your weak-topics list):
- Medium: [Coin Change II (#518)](https://leetcode.com/problems/coin-change-2/), [Pacific Atlantic Water Flow (#417)](https://leetcode.com/problems/pacific-atlantic-water-flow/), [Longest Repeating Character Replacement (#424)](https://leetcode.com/problems/longest-repeating-character-replacement/), [Daily Temperatures (#739)](https://leetcode.com/problems/daily-temperatures/).
- Hard: [Trapping Rain Water (#42)](https://leetcode.com/problems/trapping-rain-water/), [Word Break II (#140)](https://leetcode.com/problems/word-break-ii/), [Minimum Window Substring (#76)](https://leetcode.com/problems/minimum-window-substring/), [Sliding Window Maximum (#239)](https://leetcode.com/problems/sliding-window-maximum/).

### Mock protocol (mirror a real interview)

1. **Set a hard 60-minute timer.** Phone face-down, no music with lyrics.
2. **Talk out loud the entire time** — record yourself if you can. Even at home, narrate: "I'm thinking this is a sliding-window problem because…".
3. **Walk through input/output and edge cases before coding.**
4. **State Big-O before submitting.** Even if it's wrong; the interview signal is *attempting* analysis.
5. **At 60m, stop wherever you are.** Even if 80% done.

### Mock review (30 min, immediately after)

For each problem write a **one-page postmortem** in Obsidian:
- What was the *first* approach I tried? Why?
- Where did I get stuck? (specific minute mark + what I was thinking)
- What was the *optimal* approach (per editorial)? What's the gap?
- What pattern keyword should have triggered the right approach?

Tag both postmortems `#mock/day-14` so you can revisit before Day 21.

## 2. Week 2 Weak-Topic Review (60 min)

Open Obsidian, search for `#review/day-06` through `#review/day-11`. Pick the **2 topics where your notes are thinnest** — those are your gaps.

For each weak topic:
- Re-read the syllabus deep-dive in 15 minutes.
- *Without re-reading*, write the 5 recall questions from the relevant day file and answer them.
- Score yourself: any answer < 80% confident → tag the topic `#weak` and schedule another pass before Day 21.

**Likely candidates from Week 2 to watch for:**
- OAuth flows (PKCE vs Client Credentials boundary).
- RLS enforcement plumbing.
- Spring AI tool design contracts.
- HNSW tuning knobs.
- ReAct loop step bound.

## 3. Reflection (30 min)

Write 200 words in Obsidian under `#sprint/day-14-reflection`:

- Concrete change to your study plan for the next 7 days.
